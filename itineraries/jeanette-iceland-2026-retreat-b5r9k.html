The Journey

A boutique, unhurried way into Iceland.

This is the journey we keep coming back to for you. It's built around Midgard Adventure, a boutique, family-run operator in South Iceland with a warmth and personality that larger tour companies don't quite match. You'll spend four days with their small group (maximum 16), exploring the south coast at Midgard's gentle, considered pace, followed by a private day just for the two of you with the Midgard team, taking you into corners of the country that most visitors never see.

From there, you'll head west to Hotel Húsafell, surrounded by lava fields and glacier views, for three unhurried nights. This is the part of the trip we've designed with you in mind, with plenty of room to breathe, reflect, and meditate. Two final nights in Reykjavik round everything off before you fly home, giving you time to settle back into the city's creative energy.

Day 2
Thu 8 October
The Golden Circle & First Night at Midgard

Today marks the start of your Midgard Adventure. You'll be picked up from your Reykjavik hotel at 9am and spend the day visiting Iceland's iconic Golden Circle: Þingvellir National Park, where the tectonic plates quietly pull apart, the Geysir geothermal area where Strokkur erupts every few minutes, and Gullfoss, dropping in two thundering tiers into the canyon below. Lunch is at Friðheimar Tomato Farm, a working greenhouse with its own geothermal bakery, where they serve a really good tomato soup alongside fresh-baked bread. By evening you'll arrive at Midgard Base Camp in the heart of South Iceland, home for the next three nights. Dinner is waiting when you are, and afterwards the rooftop hot tub is yours whenever you'd like it.

Day 3
Fri 9 October
Katla Ice Caves & the South Coast

This morning the Midgard team takes you to Katla, Iceland's most powerful subglacial volcano, for an ice cave expedition beneath the glacier. It's a really special experience: the walls of the cave shift from black volcanic ash to translucent blue ice, and your guide will explain how the whole thing changes season to season. In the afternoon, you'll head to Reynisfjara where the Atlantic crashes against the basalt columns and the famous sea stacks rise from the black sand, with further stops along the south coast as time allows. Back at Midgard for dinner, and another evening under the dancing skies.

Day 4
Sat 10 October
Super Jeep Into Þórsmörk

A real favourite. Modified 4×4 Super Jeeps ford glacial rivers to reach Þórsmörk Mountain Reserve, a highland nature area so remote it can only be accessed with specialist vehicles. You'll spend the whole day in the reserve: wide river crossings, volcanic ridgelines, views across to Eyjafjallajökull volcano (the one that famously grounded European air travel in 2010), and the kind of quiet that only exists where there are no roads. Midgard also include a visit to a local Icelandic home along the way, which is always a highlight. Back at base for a traditional lamb dinner, and one more night of aurora watching to close out your time at Midgard.

Day 5

Glacier Walk & South Coast Waterfalls

The final day of your Midgard Adventure. You'll start with a guided hike on Sólheimajökull Glacier, crampons on, moving quietly between crevasses as your guide explains how the glacier has shifted over the years. Stop by the glacier lagoon afterwards, then three of Iceland's most beautiful waterfalls in one afternoon: Skógafoss thundering down from a grassy ridge, Seljalandsfoss where a path takes you right behind the cascade, and the hidden Gljúfrabúi tucked into a narrow canyon that most visitors walk straight past. In the evening, you'll say your goodbyes to the Midgard team and transfer to Aurora Igloo South, a glass-ceiling igloo with private bathroom, designed for Northern Lights viewing from the comfort of your bed.

Day 6
Mon 12 October
Private "Not in Your Guidebook" Adventure, then West to Húsafell

Wake up under the glass ceiling for one more look at the sky, and then step into what might be the most personal day of the whole trip. The Midgard team calls this their "Not in Your Guidebook" adventure, and they mean it literally: most of the places you'll visit aren't mentioned in any guidebook. These are the places the Midgard guides go on their own days off, where you're unlikely to see anyone other than a local traveller or a farmer. It's a truly local experience that only Midgard offers.

The area is in and around Fljótshlíð, the "hill by the river", a beautiful and peaceful stretch of countryside where many of the Icelandic Sagas took place. Depending on what the day calls for, your guide might take you to the small canyons and favourite playgrounds underneath Mt. Þórólfsfell, the 120-metre summit of Stóri Dímon for the view (or a walk around its base for hidden corners), rivers and hidden waterfalls around Mt. Þríhyrningur, and one of Iceland's few small forests where a farmer lived to 100 and an old well still stands. Taking a sip from that well is Midgard's way of staying young, and they say it could become yours too. In the afternoon, a private transfer takes you west to Hotel Húsafell for three unhurried nights.

Slowing Down at Húsafell

This is the day we've been building up to. No schedule, no agenda, nowhere you need to be. Hraunfossar and Barnafoss are five minutes from the hotel, a long line of springs emerging directly from the lava field and cascading into the river below. The surrounding lava fields have hiking trails right from the hotel, perfect for a morning walk out toward the Langjökull glacier views. In the afternoon, continue exploring on foot, or return to the hotel for a long read by the fire. This is the part of the trip designed for you to breathe.

A Day for the Baths

Today is for soaking. You have a wonderful choice to make: the Hotel Húsafell Geothermal Pool right on the hotel grounds, or the Húsafell Canyon Baths, a short walk away through the surrounding nature. The canyon baths are the real highlight here, a set of soaking pools built into the canyon itself using locally sourced stone, with temperatures ranging from 30 to 41°C. A guided walk takes you to the two-tiered Langifoss waterfall first, then down to the baths for a serene soak in the canyon. Both options are genuinely wonderful, and if you can't choose, you don't have to, do both. If you're feeling up to it, add in an easy afternoon walk before settling down for dinner.

Reykjavik at Leisure

A full free day in the capital, which means it's whatever you want it to be. A few suggestions: Hallgrímskirkja church, whose architecture echoes the basalt columns you'll have seen at Reynisfjara. Harpa Concert Hall on the harbour, all geometric glass and shifting colour. Sundhöllin, the city's oldest geothermal pool, open late and worth a visit on a crisp October afternoon. And if you'd like us to book a restaurant for you for the evening, just say the word and we'll sort it out.
