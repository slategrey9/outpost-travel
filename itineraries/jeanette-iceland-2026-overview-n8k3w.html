The Proposal

One country, three very different journeys.

October is a wonderful time to visit Iceland. The evenings grow long, the first Northern Lights of the season begin to dance across the sky, and the countryside eases into its quiet winter rhythm. It's a season that rewards stillness and attention in equal measure, and it's a real joy to be putting something together for you.

We've shaped three journeys for you, each with its own feel and pace, so you can see which one sits closest to the trip you're imagining. Take your time browsing, and click through to any option that catches your eye for the full day-by-day, property notes, and everything that's included.

Option A

The Guided Classic

Nordic Visitor small group tour, with your own extensions

11 nights Flexible Thursday departures Small group to 16

A lovely way to see Iceland's greatest hits, starting with a 7-day small group tour from Nordic Visitor, one of the country's most trusted and established operators. The tour takes you through the Golden Circle, Snæfellsnes, the dramatic south coast, and the glacier lagoon, before dropping you back in Reykjavik. From there, we've added a semi-private Þórsmörk day trip (just four guests) and two quiet nights out at Hotel Húsafell in West Iceland to round off the trip at a slower pace.

Option B

The Immersive Retreat

Midgard Adventure, Aurora Igloo, and Hotel Húsafell

11 nights 7 – 18 Oct Small group to 16

This one takes you gently off the beaten path, and into the hands of Midgard Adventure, a boutique family-run operator with a real personal touch. You'll spend four nights at their Midgard Base Camp in South Iceland, joining their small group Northern Lights Adventure, before a private day exploring the corners of the country most visitors never reach. After a night in a glass-ceiling aurora igloo, you'll head west to Hotel Húsafell for three unhurried nights surrounded by lava fields and quiet, with all the room you need to slow down, breathe, and meditate, before two final nights back in Reykjavik.

Option C

Iceland, Yours Alone

A fully private journey, just the two of you

10 nights Flexible dates in October Fully private

Ten nights of completely private travel, just the two of you, with your own driver-guide and vehicle on the days that call for it. You'll settle into three carefully chosen bases: Reykjavik for its creative energy, Reykholt in the west for its geothermal calm, and Vík for the dramatic southern coastline. Every touring day is entirely yours to shape, with the freedom to linger when something catches you, slow down when you feel like it, or follow the weather wherever it takes you.

Our Recommendation

Where we'd gently point you

All three are great choices, and the right one really comes down to what matters most to you for this particular trip.

Based on everything you shared with us, Option B, the Immersive Retreat, feels like the closest fit. Midgard is a genuinely boutique, family-run operator with a warmth and personality you don't find with the larger tour companies, and the itinerary takes you off the beaten path in a way that the more iconic routes simply don't. Most importantly, the pace of this journey gives you real time to breathe, reflect, and settle into each place, which is exactly the kind of space you mentioned you'd love for meditation and simply being present.

If Option B doesn't work for scheduling reasons, Option A, the Guided Classic, is where we'd look next. It covers more of the iconic sights, and because Nordic Visitor depart every Thursday through October, we have the flexibility of three possible start dates rather than being locked into one window. That gives you a little more room to travel later in the month if that works better for you.
