The Journey

The iconic sights, thoughtfully handled.

This is the journey for you if you'd like Iceland's most famous places handled by one of the country's most established operators. The heart of it is Nordic Visitor's 7-day Best of South & West Iceland winter tour, a small group programme capped at 16 guests, covering the Golden Circle, the Snæfellsnes Peninsula, the dramatic south coast, and the glacier lagoon. Everything logistical is taken care of for you, all you need to do is show up and take it all in.

Once the tour returns to Reykjavik, we've added two extensions of our own: a semi-private Þórsmörk day trip with a maximum of four guests, and two quiet nights at Hotel Húsafell in West Iceland to round off the trip at a gentler pace. A final night at a Keflavik airport hotel makes the morning flight home that little bit easier.

Arrival & Reykjavik

Day 2 · Nordic Visitor Tour Day 2
Fri 9 October
Wonders of Snæfellsnes

The Nordic Visitor tour gets going today with a full day on the Snæfellsnes Peninsula, often called "Iceland in miniature" for the variety of landscapes packed into one small stretch of the country. You'll visit the fishing village of Arnarstapi with its dramatic coastal cliffs, the towering Lóndrangar sea stacks, the black pebbles of Djúpalónssandur beach, and the much-photographed Kirkjufell, one of the most distinctive mountains in Iceland. Evening check-in at Hótel Hamar, where the Nordic Visitor group gathers for a welcome dinner.

Day 3 · Nordic Visitor Tour Day 3
Sat 10 October
The Golden Circle & Friðheimar Farm

Iceland's most famous route, done properly. You'll start at Þingvellir National Park (a UNESCO site and the birthplace of the world's oldest surviving parliament), where you can quite literally walk between the North American and Eurasian tectonic plates. Then on to the geothermal field at Geysir, where Strokkur erupts every few minutes, and Gullfoss, dropping in two thundering tiers into the canyon below. A stop at Friðheimar Tomato Farm follows, a working greenhouse where they grow tomatoes geothermally and bake fresh bread on site. By evening, check-in at Hotel South Coast in Selfoss.

Day 4 · Nordic Visitor Tour Day 4
Sun 11 October
South Coast Waterfalls & Black Sand

The south coast in all its drama. You'll start the day at Seljalandsfoss, where a path behind the cascade takes you inside the waterfall itself. Then on along the Eyjafjöll mountains and past rural farmlands until you reach Reynisfjara, the famous black sand beach backed by towering basalt columns and the Reynisdrangar sea stacks rising from the sea. A seasonal ice cave experience is included on October departures, adding a genuine Iceland highlight to the day. Evening at the Magma Hotel, a wonderful small property with lakeside glacier views.

Day 5 · Nordic Visitor Tour Day 5
Mon 12 October
Glacier Lagoon & Diamond Beach

One of those days that stays with you for a long time. You'll head east to Vatnajökull, Europe's largest glacier, for the drive through some of the most cinematic landscapes Iceland has to offer. The highlight is Jökulsárlón Glacier Lagoon, where huge luminous blue icebergs calve from the glacier and drift slowly toward the sea. Then Diamond Beach, just across the road, where those same icebergs wash ashore on black volcanic sand and glitter in the October light. Back to the Magma Hotel for a second night.

Day 6 · Nordic Visitor Tour Day 6
Tue 13 October
Return Along the South Shore

The return leg toward Reykjavik, with a few more stops that reward the time. Skógafoss is first, one of the tallest waterfalls in Iceland and genuinely thundering. Then the Skógar Folk Museum, a really well-curated collection of turf farmhouses, fishing boats, and tools that show centuries of hard living on this coast. Further along, a stop at the Lava Centre, where interactive exhibits make the geology of Iceland feel visceral rather than abstract. Evening check-in at Hótel Skuggi back in Reykjavik.

Day 8 · Semi-Private Day Tour
Thu 15 October
Þórsmörk Super Jeep Day Trip

A semi-private day tour into Þórsmörk Mountain Reserve, capped at four guests in a modified Super Jeep, giving you a very different experience from the larger full-day group excursions. You'll depart Reykjavik early and head into the highland reserve via river crossings that only specialist vehicles can manage, spending most of the day in the valley. Volcanic ridgelines, wide river beds, views toward Eyjafjallajökull, and a quiet that only exists where there are no roads. Return to Reykjavik in the evening.

Day 9

West to Hotel Húsafell

Check out of ION City after a slow morning in Reykjavik, and a private transfer takes you west to Hotel Húsafell, around two hours through the lava fields. This is where the pace really drops. Húsafell sits surrounded by lava fields and glacier views in a genuinely quiet corner of West Iceland. You'll arrive in time for lunch, with the rest of the afternoon yours to settle in, enjoy the hotel's geothermal pool, or take a short walk to the Hraunfossar lava waterfalls five minutes away.

Day 10
Sat 17 October
A Full Day at Húsafell

A full day at Hotel Húsafell, and genuinely no obligations. The real highlight here is the Húsafell Canyon Baths, a short walk away through the surrounding nature, a set of soaking pools built into the canyon itself using locally sourced stone with temperatures from 30 to 41°C. A guided walk takes you down past the two-tiered Langifoss waterfall first. If the baths aren't calling, the hotel's own geothermal pool is right on site and equally wonderful. And if you're feeling up to it, there are easy walks from the hotel toward the Langjökull glacier views to fill the afternoon.

Optional Add-On

Into the Glacier ice cave tour. A guided expedition into a man-made tunnel carved deep inside the Langjökull ice cap, one of the more unusual experiences in Iceland. We can arrange this as a half-day add-on if you'd like.
