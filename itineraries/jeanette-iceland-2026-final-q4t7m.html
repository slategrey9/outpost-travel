Day 2
Thu 8 October
The Golden Circle & First Night at Midgard

Today marks the start of your Midgard Adventure. You'll be picked up from your Reykjavík hotel at 9am and spend the day visiting Iceland's iconic Golden Circle: Þingvellir National Park, where the tectonic plates quietly pull apart, the Geysir geothermal area where Strokkur erupts every few minutes, and Gullfoss, dropping in two thundering tiers into the canyon below. Lunch is at Friðheimar Tomato Farm, a working greenhouse with its own geothermal bakery, where they serve a really good tomato soup alongside fresh-baked bread. By evening you'll arrive at Midgard Base Camp in the heart of South Iceland, home for the next three nights. Dinner is waiting when you are, and afterwards the rooftop hot tub is yours whenever you'd like it.

Day 3
Fri 9 October
Katla Ice Caves & the South Coast

This morning the Midgard team takes you to Katla, Iceland's most powerful subglacial volcano, for an ice cave expedition beneath the glacier. The walls of the cave shift from black volcanic ash to translucent blue ice, and your guide will explain how the whole thing changes season to season. In the afternoon, you'll head to Reynisfjara where the Atlantic crashes against the basalt columns and the famous sea stacks rise from the black sand, with further stops along the south coast as time allows. Back at Midgard for dinner, and another evening under the dancing skies.

Day 4
Sat 10 October
Super Jeep Into Þórsmörk

A real favourite. Modified 4×4 Super Jeeps ford glacial rivers to reach Þórsmörk Mountain Reserve, a highland nature area so remote it can only be accessed with specialist vehicles. You'll spend the whole day in the reserve: wide river crossings, volcanic ridgelines, views across to Eyjafjallajökull volcano (the one that famously grounded European air travel in 2010), and the kind of quiet that only exists where there are no roads. Midgard also include a visit to a local Icelandic home along the way, which is always a highlight. Back at base for a traditional lamb dinner, and one more night of aurora watching to close out your time at Midgard.

Day 5
Sun 11 October
Glacier Walk & South Coast Waterfalls

The final day of your Midgard Adventure. You'll start with a guided hike on Sólheimajökull Glacier, crampons on, moving quietly between crevasses as your guide explains how the glacier has shifted over the years. Stop by the glacier lagoon afterwards, then three of Iceland's most beautiful waterfalls in one afternoon: Skógafoss thundering down from a grassy ridge, Seljalandsfoss where a path takes you right behind the cascade, and the hidden Gljúfrabúi tucked into a narrow canyon that most visitors walk straight past. In the evening, you'll say your goodbyes to the Midgard team and arrive at Aurora Igloo South, a glass-ceiling igloo with private bathroom, designed for Northern Lights viewing from the comfort of your bed.

Day 6
Mon 12 October
Private "Not in Your Guidebook" Adventure

Wake up under the glass ceiling for one more look at the sky, and then step into what might be the most personal day of the whole trip. The Midgard team calls this their "Not in Your Guidebook" adventure, and they mean it literally: most of the places you'll visit aren't mentioned in any guidebook. These are the places the Midgard guides go on their own days off, where you're unlikely to see anyone other than a local traveller or a farmer. It's a truly local experience that only Midgard offers.

The area is in and around Fljótshlíð, the "hill by the river", a beautiful and peaceful stretch of countryside where many of the Icelandic Sagas took place. Depending on what the day calls for, your guide might take you to the small canyons and favourite playgrounds underneath Mt. Þórólfsfell, the 120-metre summit of Stóri Dímon for the view (or a walk around its base for hidden corners), rivers and hidden waterfalls around Mt. Þríhyrningur, and one of Iceland's few small forests where a farmer lived to 100 and an old well still stands. Taking a sip from that well is Midgard's way of staying young, and they say it could become yours too. In the evening you'll be dropped back in Reykjavík for two nights at Sands Hotel.

Day 7
Tue 13 October
Reykjavík at Leisure

A full free day in the capital, which means it's whatever you want it to be. A few suggestions: Hallgrímskirkja Church, whose architecture echoes the basalt columns you'll have seen at Reynisfjara. Harpa Concert Hall on the harbour, all geometric glass and shifting colour. A walk around the Old Harbour, the historic working edge of the city, still home to fishing boats and whale-watching operators. And the Reykjavík Maritime Museum, which sits right on the harbour and tells the story of Iceland's relationship with the sea. If you'd like us to book a restaurant for you for the evening, just say the word.

Day 8
Wed 14 October
West to Húsafell

A leisurely morning in Reykjavík, a last walk through the city, and then a private transfer west to Hotel Húsafell, about two hours through the lava fields. You'll arrive with the afternoon still ahead. Hraunfossar and Barnafoss are five minutes from the hotel, a long line of springs emerging directly from the lava field and cascading into the river below. Settle in, walk out into the surrounding nature, or simply sit by the fire and watch the light shift.

Tucked away from the city with very little light pollution, the hotel sits in one of the best spots in West Iceland for Northern Lights, and you'll have a good chance of seeing them from the comfort of your room or the lounge. If the aurora appears after you've turned in for the night, the hotel offers complimentary wake-up calls so you don't miss it.

Day 10
Fri 16 October
A Day for the Baths

Today is for soaking. You have a wonderful choice to make: the Hotel Húsafell Geothermal Pool right on the hotel grounds, or the Húsafell Canyon Baths, a short walk away through the surrounding nature. The canyon baths are the real highlight here, a set of soaking pools built into the canyon itself using locally sourced stone, with temperatures ranging from 30 to 41°C. A guided walk takes you to the two-tiered Langifoss waterfall first, then down to the baths for a serene soak in the canyon. Both options are genuinely wonderful, and if you can't choose, you don't have to, do both.
