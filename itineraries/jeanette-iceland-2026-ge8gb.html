The full itinerary

The Journey

A private circle through fire and ice.

Ten nights of completely private travel, just the two of you. You'll begin with an easy first night at ION City Hotel in Reykjavik to settle in after the long flight, then head out onto the road with your own driver-guide and vehicle on the days that call for it. The rest of the journey flows through three carefully chosen bases: the geothermal calm of Reykholt in Borgarfjörður, the dramatic coastline at Vík, and the creative energy of Reykjavik on your return. Every touring day is yours to shape, with the freedom to linger when something catches you, slow down when you feel like it, or follow the weather wherever it takes you.

The rhythm of the trip is built around four full private touring days, interspersed with genuinely unhurried days at each base. You can spend those between-days hiking, soaking in a geothermal pool, or simply doing nothing at all, whichever feels right in the moment.

Day 2 · Private Driver-Guide
Wed 21 October
Reykjavik to Reykholt via Whale Fjord

Your driver-guide picks you up from ION City this morning and heads north into West Iceland. The route passes through Hvalfjörður, meaning "Whale Fjord", a stunning stretch of steep mountains, lush valleys, and waterfalls that once played a strategic role as a naval base during WWII. Further on, you'll stop at Hraunfossar, a 900-metre stretch of small waterfalls pouring out of the lava field, and Barnafoss right beside it, a narrow rocky gorge with its own folk tale. Arrive at Fosshotel Reykholt by late afternoon and settle in for three unhurried nights.

Hiking in the Borgarfjörður Countryside

A day without a schedule. Fosshotel Reykholt sits in the scenic Borgarfjörður countryside, and there are hiking trails winding out from the hotel through the surrounding lava fields and low hills. The hotel's wellness area has outdoor hot and cold tubs, a sauna, and a steam bath, so if the weather turns you can happily stay put. October light in this valley has a particular quality, and the pace of the day is entirely yours.

A Slow Day at Reykholt

A second free day, designed for whatever you feel like. Take a taxi to Krauma Geothermal Bath, just seven minutes from the hotel, for a proper Icelandic spa afternoon. Return to Hraunfossar if you want them in different light, wander the lava fields, or read by the fire. The Fosshotel restaurant serves Icelandic and international dishes made with locally sourced ingredients, so evenings are easy too. This is the part of the trip that gives everything else room to settle.

Krauma Geothermal Bath (7 min by taxi). A modern bath house built around the Deildartunguhver hot spring, Europe's most powerful. Five different pools at varying temperatures, a steam bath, and a relaxation room with a fire. Entrance and taxi not included, both easy to arrange on the day.

Day 5 · Private Driver-Guide
Sat 24 October
Þingvellir, the South Coast & Vík

Your driver-guide picks you up for the southward journey. First stop is Þingvellir National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in a rift valley between the North American and Eurasian tectonic plates, and the site of the world's oldest surviving parliament, established in 930 AD. Then Urriðafoss, one of the widest waterfalls in Iceland with an impressive flow rate. Further along, Seljalandsfoss, 65 metres high, where a path behind the cascade takes you inside the waterfall itself. Finally Gljúfrabúi, the "Canyon Dweller", a hidden waterfall tucked behind a narrow canyon of moss-covered cliffs that most visitors walk straight past. Arrive at Hotel Vík by evening.

Day 6 · At Leisure
Sun 25 October
Vík & the Hidden Valley of Þakgil

Hidden deep in the landscapes of South Iceland, Þakgil is a secluded valley surrounded by rugged mountains, moss-covered lava formations, and glacial rivers. Far from the main routes, it has a rare sense of isolation, striking contrasts of dark volcanic terrain softened by vibrant greenery, and the kind of quiet that invites slow hikes and still moments. Spend the day exploring here at your own pace, with two lovely optional tours on offer if you want something more active.

Day 7 · Private Driver-Guide
Mon 26 October
Canyons, Glacier Lagoon & Diamond Beach

One of those days that stays with you. Drive east past Gígjagjá, also known as the Yoda Cave, a naturally formed cave with an opening that resembles the silhouette of Yoda. Through Eldhraun, one of the largest lava fields in the world at around 565 km², covered in a thick layer of green moss that creates a surreal landscape, formed during the Laki eruption of 1783. Fjaðrárgljúfur Canyon follows, about two kilometres long and up to 100 metres deep, sculpted over millennia by the Fjaðrá River. Then Jökulsárlón Glacier Lagoon, where luminous blue icebergs drift slowly toward the sea beneath Vatnajökull Glacier, and Diamond Beach just across the road, where those same icebergs wash ashore on black sand and glitter in the October light.

Day 8 · Private Driver-Guide
Tue 27 October
Reynisfjara, Skógafoss & Back to the City

A final morning in the south before heading west to Reykjavik. Reynisfjara Beach stretches away with its black sand backed by the columnar basalt formations of Reynisfjall Mountain and the Reynisdrangar sea stacks rising 66 metres from the sea. Skógafoss follows, one of Iceland's tallest waterfalls at 60 metres high and 25 metres wide, with rainbows often visible in the mist on sunny days. By afternoon you're back in Reykjavik, checking into the Sand Hotel on Laugavegur, the city's main street, well placed for the harbour, the restaurants, and Reykjavik's creative pulse.

Day 9 · Reykjavik On Your Own
Wed 28 October
The City, at Your Own Pace

A full day to explore Reykjavik however you'd like. Hallgrímskirkja rises 73 metres above the city, its architecture meant to echo the basalt lava flows you'll have seen earlier in the trip. Harpa Concert Hall, designed by Ólafur Elíasson, is a steel framework filled with geometric glass panels that constantly shift in colour. The Sun Voyager sculpture faces the setting sun over Faxaflói Bay. Höfði House is the site of the 1986 summit between Reagan and Gorbachev. Alþingishúsið is the historic parliament building, completed in 1881. And the charming streets of Reykjavik's Old Town, with its colourful houses and cosy cafés, reward unhurried wandering.
