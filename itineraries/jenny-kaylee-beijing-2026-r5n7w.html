Lama Temple & the Forbidden City

A quick word on today's guide first. We've specially sourced Daisy for this trip, and we think she'll feel like the right match for Jenny and Kaylee after Wutaishan. Her own long practice of Mahayana Buddhism and her knowledge of Chinese history will bring both of today's sites to life, particularly the Lama Temple. There's more on her background further down the page.

The morning is spent at the Lama Temple, also known as Yonghegong. With its sloping orange-tiled eaves juxtaposed against an elevated ring road in central Beijing, Yonghegong has been at the heart of Tibetan Buddhism in the capital for over three centuries. You'll navigate through drifting incense smoke and past the quiet prayer halls toward the Hall of Boundless Happiness, home to a 26-metre Maitreya Buddha carved from a single piece of white sandalwood, which holds a Guinness World Record of its own.

After lunch, step inside the Forbidden City, the massive imperial palace that was once home to China's emperors. Its gates remained closed to all but the royal household for nearly 600 years, until 1924, when a warlord expelled the last emperor and the site was finally opened to the world. From the Meridian Gate through the three great halls and beyond, Daisy will give you an insider's tour at an unhurried pace. Time permitting, a short stroll through the nearby hutong lanes before the day ends at around 5:00 PM.

Jinshanling Great Wall

Monday, 1 June 2026

The Great Wall at Jinshanling

Rising from the sea in the east before extending 5,000 miles to the deserts of China's far west, the Great Wall is arguably the greatest man-made wonder on earth. For your hike today, we bypass the more touristy sections and take you straight to the stunning Ming Dynasty ruins outside Beijing, where lonely guards far from home once kept a weary watch over the barbarian lands beyond.

Two and a half hours from Beijing, in the rugged mountains of Hebei Province, lies one of the most picturesque sections of the Wall within reach of the capital. Free from the crowds of its closer neighbours, Jinshanling's 10.5-kilometre stretch of both restored and wild wall is steeped in centuries of history. A short gondola ride up the mountain, followed by a three-hour hike, opens onto an unbelievable view of countless watchtowers stacked against endless mountain silhouettes, and a window into the minds of those ancient soldiers who once shared this view many hundreds of years before.

Optional Add-On  ·  Tai Chi on the Great Wall

If you'd like a moment of stillness to balance the hike, we can arrange a private Tai Chi session on the wall with Master Eric Liu. Eric is the founder of the Beijing Sanfeng Tai Chi Club, a seventh-generation heir of the Imperial Yang-style tradition (Lao Liu Lu), a member of the Chinese Wushu Association, and the resident Tai Chi Master at Mandarin Oriental Qianmen, Beijing.

Eric's focus is on song gong, the gentler "relaxation" qigong that draws on the Book of Changes and the Tao Te Ching. As he puts it: "Tai Chi lies in everywhere of our lives. Softness, balance and effortless yielding are the dominant power, not force or extremity." A short session on the wall, held before or after the hike, is a lovely way to bring the day into focus.

Additional cost: approx. SGD 400, subject to Master Liu's availability.
